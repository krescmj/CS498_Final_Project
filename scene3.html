Major League Baseball - Team Performance Versus Player Payroll

Those who follow Major League Baseball know that it is common place for professional players to be paid several million dollars a year. For most of the season, the number of players allowed on a roster is 25 players. This number may not seem like a lot, but this puts the total payroll for the players on a typical team in tens of milions of dollars, sometimes even higher. Does this investment payoff? Are the teams who have the highest payroll the most successful? This visualization on this website is intended to help answer those questions. It also provides an an avenue for the user to further investigate other questions which might be raised while interacting.


All of data for this website was taken from stevethehump.com and mlb.com.


The implementation toolset for all the pages in this visualization is Bootstrap, HTML, CSS, Javascript and D3. All of the templates and javascript library's which were utilized during the creation of this visualization on the website are distributed under the GNU General Public License.

About the Visualization

This visualization follows the martini glass interactive structure. Each scene shows how an individual team performs given their total player payroll investment over the last 10 years. Each visualization was built using the d3.js javascript library. Each slide has a section that describes how that specific visualization effectively utilizes scenes, annotations, parameters and triggers.
